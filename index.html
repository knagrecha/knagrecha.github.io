BIO

Kabir Nagrecha is a 17-year old incoming PhD student at University of California, San Diego. He grew up in Reading, England, and came to sunny California in 2012. His research interests center on improving the machine learning pipeline from design to deployment. To this end, he leverages techniques from database management, systems design, and HCI.

Kabir Nagrecha recently finished his undergraduate degree at UC San Diego, where he conducted research on machine learning systems such as Krypton and Hydra (ongoing). He has received recognition for his work from the Computing Research Association and the UC San Diego Computer Science Department through their respective Outstanding Undergraduate Researcher Awards.

Currently, he is in the final round of the ACM SIGMOD Student Research Competition, and working on Hydra, an advanced system for model parallel training that offers dramatic speedups compared to the state-of-the-art.
